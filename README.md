# Inventory Optimization
## Problem Statement
Our business currently has ~$61.M tied up in excess Packaging and Chemicals inventory, which significantly restricts our free cash flow. The goal is to reduce this frozen capital by over $12M, bringing our total category exposure down to under $50M without risking stockouts.

_Which months show the lowest customer demand for Packaging and Chemicals, allowing us to safely cut back our order sizes and free up that $12M?_  
## About the data
Dataset Overview: Supply Chain & Inventory OperationsThis dataset contains transactional and master data for an organization's supply chain network. It tracks 500 unique products (SKUs) across 3 physical warehouses, monitoring historical customer demand alongside daily warehouse inventory movements.The dataset is spread across two primary operational layers:1. The Core Analytical Sheets (Used for this Project)These sheets contain the historical timelines and financial figures needed to optimize our inventory and free up frozen capital:Daily_Demand (10,000 Rows):What it is: A comprehensive log tracking exactly how many units of each product customers requested per day.Business Value: This serves as our source of truth for identifying seasonal customer buying waves and finding the "slow months."Product_Master (500 Rows):What it is: A catalog of every product handled by the company, detailing its broader category (such as Packaging and Chemicals) and its wholesale unit cost.Business Value: This allows us to convert physical stock volume into actual corporate dollars ($\$$) to isolate exactly where cash flow is bottlenecked.2. Supporting Network SheetsThese sheets provide operational context regarding how inventory moves through the supply chain and where it physically sits:Inventory_Transactions (5,000 Rows):What it is: A ledger of warehouse floor activities tracking inventory coming in from suppliers (IN) and inventory leaving to fulfill orders (OUT), while monitoring the remaining safety stock levels.Warehouse_Master (3 Rows):What it is: A structural table defining the three distribution hubs (WH1, WH2, WH3) and their maximum physical storage capacity thresholds.Supplier_Master (50 Rows):What it is: A vendor profile list tracking the names, geographic locations, contractual agreements, and performance ratings of the company's third-party suppliers.
